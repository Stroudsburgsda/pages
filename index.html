Restoration Hour of Prayer (Upcoming Quarter)

Women’s Ministry is planning a powerful and deeply meaningful quarter. The Restoration Hour of Prayer will continue to cover the important topic of “Church Hurt.”

Understanding the Reality

“Church hurt” refers to the emotional, psychological, or spiritual harm inflicted by church leaders or fellow believers. It can leave deep wounds, but recognizing your pain is valid is the first step toward healing.

Sources of Church Hurt:
  • Misuse of Spiritual Authority: Leaders using scripture or spiritual language to control, shame, or exploit others.
  • Relational Harm: Gossip, betrayal, exclusion, or failure of the church to support you in a crisis.
  • Toxic Church Culture: Prioritizing numerical growth over people or allowing immature/unhealthy patterns to go unchecked.
Steps for Healing:
  • Grieve & Detangle: Allow yourself to feel the pain. It is important to separate your personal faith and view of God from the imperfect, fallible humans who caused your pain.
  • Set Boundaries: You have the right to step away from a toxic environment or take a break from attending services to protect your mental health.
  • Seek Support: Healing is rarely a solo journey. Speaking with a licensed mental health professional can help you process religious trauma or deep-seated emotional wounds.

Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 6/20/2026

Sharing Him: Your Life Speaks Before Your Words

Every day, whether we realize it or not, we are telling a story. Through our words, our reactions, our patience, our kindness, or even our frustration, we reveal something about the God we claim to serve.

This week’s lesson, “Share Him,” reminds us that witnessing is not simply about giving Bible studies or preaching sermons. Often, it begins long before that. It begins in the ordinary moments of life. Someone is always watching, not for our perfection, but for evidence of Jesus in us. That’s the heart of witnessing.

Overflow of the Heart

The Great Commission was never reserved for pastors, Bible workers, or extroverts. It is a calling for every believer. Effective witnessing doesn’t flow from pressure; it flows from overflow. When God’s love fills our hearts, sharing Him becomes a natural response. We speak because we’ve experienced Him. We love because He first loved us.

Peter and John said it best: “We cannot but speak the things which we have seen and heard.” Their witness wasn’t built on polished arguments but on personal experience. They had been with Jesus, and it showed. The real question for us is, can people tell we’ve been with Jesus?

The lesson also reminds us that sharing Christ must be marked by compassion, patience, and authenticity. We don’t force faith. We reflect it. We build relationships. We pray for opportunities. We listen. We love. And we trust the Holy Spirit to do the work only He can do.

As you continue to study this week, ask God not just to teach you how to witness, but to shape you into someone whose life makes others curious about Him. Sometimes the most powerful sermon you will ever preach is the one you live.

Sabbath Discussion Prep

Food for Thought:

"If someone were to observe your life this week: your words, reactions, and relationships, what picture of Jesus would they walk away with, and how does that shape your understanding of what it means to “share Him”?"

Religious Liberty

When Heaven Went Silent

God Was Preparing the Way Then. Is He Preparing the Way Now?

“Behold, I will send my messenger, and he shall prepare the way before me…”

— Malachi 3:1

When the book of Malachi closes, something remarkable happens. Heaven goes silent. For approximately four hundred years, no prophet arises in Israel. No new Scripture is written. No divine messenger appears with a fresh word from the Lord. To many, it may have seemed that God had stopped working. But God was not absent. He was preparing.

While heaven appeared silent, history was moving exactly according to God’s plan. Empires rose and fell. The Greek language spread across much of the known world, creating a common means of communication. The Scriptures were translated into Greek, making God’s Word accessible to millions. Synagogues were established throughout the Mediterranean region, providing centers where people gathered to study Scripture and discuss spiritual matters. Then Rome emerged. The Romans built roads, trade routes, and transportation networks unlike anything the world had previously seen. Cities became connected. Travel became easier. Communication became faster.

The roads were not built for the gospel. Yet God would use them for the gospel. When John the Baptist appeared, the way had been prepared. When Jesus began His ministry, the way had been prepared. When the disciples were commissioned to carry the gospel to the world, the way had been prepared.

The same roads used by merchants and soldiers became highways for the message of salvation. Paul traveled from city to city. Churches were planted. Letters circulated among believers. The gospel spread throughout the Roman Empire with remarkable speed. What appeared to be silence was actually preparation. The Romans built roads for commerce. God used them for the gospel. Today, God has placed new roads before us. On July 4, we step onto those roads with a message of hope for a world that desperately needs it.

Modern Highways of Truth

As we approach our community outreach on July 4, we would do well to consider what God has placed in our hands. The early church had Roman roads. We have highways, automobiles, smartphones, websites, social media, livestreams, email, text messaging, and instant communication with people around the world.

Never before has God’s church had access to so many avenues for sharing truth. The challenge facing us is not a lack of opportunity. The challenge is whether we will use the opportunities God has already provided.

As Seventh-day Adventists, we believe God has entrusted us with a message of hope for these last days. The Three Angels’ Messages are not intended to remain within the walls of our church. They are meant to reach every person possible before Christ returns. On July 4, we are not simply handing out literature. We are stepping onto the roads God has placed before us. Every conversation, every flyer, every prayer offered, every invitation extended may be the very thing the Holy Spirit uses to reach a searching heart.

Malachi’s generation was called to prepare the way for Christ’s first coming. We have been called to help prepare the way for His second coming. Perhaps God is doing today what He did during those four hundred years of silence. Perhaps He has been preparing communities, opening hearts, creating opportunities, and building pathways for the gospel that we cannot yet fully see. The roads are already built. The question is whether we will walk them. On July 4, let us answer that question together. The King is coming.
