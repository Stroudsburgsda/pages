Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 5/9/2026

Prayer - That Changes Everything

What happens when prayer moves from routine to relationship? In this week’s lesson, “Prayer Warriors,” we look closely at the lives of Moses, Enoch, and Daniel. They didn’t just talk about prayer; they lived it. We also discover that prayer is not an accessory to the Christian life; it is the lifeline.

In crisis and in the ordinary rhythm of life Daniel prayed. He prayed consistently, openly, and without compromise. Enoch’s entire life is summarized in one powerful phrase: “he walked with God.” Moses was a leader, intercessor, and friend of God. His life reminds us that prayer is not only about personal connection, but it’s also about intercession and influence.

Relational & Transformative

Prayer doesn’t just change situations, it changes people. It deepens trust, clarifies purpose, strengthens faith, and aligns our hearts with God’s will. This week’s study is an invitation to become a people of prayer—to move beyond rushed words and into meaningful conversation.

Question of the Week

According to the lesson on 'The Posture of Prayer,' what is the significance of kneeling?

A. It is a posture primarily intended to show superiority over others.
B. It acts as a physical declaration to the powers of darkness that the individual chooses God.
C. It is the only posture that the Bible identifies as acceptable.
D. It is a requirement for those who wish to have their prayers heard.
Correct Answer: B
Food for Thought:

Which aspect of the prayer lives of Daniel, Enoch, or Moses do you most need to develop right now, and what specific step will you take this week to strengthen it?

Stewardship Spotlight

Where Does It Go?

The Structure of Giving

All Tithe is sent to the Pennsylvania Conference to support pastors, evangelism, and broader conference ministry.

Offerings (Combined Budget) stay right here to support our local church needs.

The Combined Budget supports utilities, Sabbath meals, quarterlies, departmental ministries, and day-to-day operations. Returning tithe is an act of obedience, while giving offering is an invitation to support His house locally.

A Shared Responsibility

If we return tithe but do not give offering, the local church does not have the resources needed to function and grow. When we give, we create space for ministry to happen right here in our community.

"Stewardship is not just about what we give — it’s about who we trust."
