Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 7/18/2026

Unity in Christ: What Kind of Church Are We Becoming?

This week, we’re studying the topic Unity in Christ. As we go through the study, it’s worth asking ourselves a simple but searching question: What kind of church are we becoming?

Paul's first letter to the Corinthians reminds us that one of the greatest threats to the church isn't always persecution from the outside; it can be division from within. The church in Corinth wasn't divided over doctrine alone. Personal preferences, favorite leaders, pride, and competition had quietly become more important than their shared identity in Christ. The result was fractured relationships and a weakened witness.

Is Corinth Closer Than We Think?

If we're honest, Corinth doesn't feel all that distant. Corinth almost feels like Stroudsburg. It's easy to rally around people, ministries, traditions, or worship styles. We can become passionate about being "right" while forgetting that Jesus prayed for His followers to be one. Unity doesn't require that we all think alike or express our faith in identical ways. Rather, biblical unity means that Christ remains at the center, shaping our attitudes, our conversations, and our relationships.

This week's lesson invites us to examine not only what we believe but how we treat one another. Paul redirects the Corinthians away from human leaders and back to the One who was crucified for them. The church is strongest when Christ is the focus and every member understands that we are part of one body with different gifts, serving the same Savior.

The lesson also embedded a powerful reminder about spiritual maturity. Mature believers don't measure success by popularity or influence. Instead, they embrace the "mind of Christ," a life marked by humility, self-sacrifice, and servant leadership. The Cross isn't just the means of our salvation; it becomes the pattern for how we live with one another every day.

The world is searching for authentic community. One of the greatest testimonies the church can offer isn't perfect agreement on every preference; it's a community of believers who genuinely love one another because Jesus has transformed them. As we continue to walk through this new quarter together, may our study do more than increase our biblical knowledge. May it reshape our hearts, deepen our love for Christ, and strengthen the bonds that unite His family.

Sabbath Discussion Prep

Food for Thought:

"Growing in Christ isn't simply about accumulating biblical knowledge; it's about allowing that knowledge to transform the way we think, serve, and love others. How is your spiritual maturity strengthening the unity of Christ's body rather than contributing to its divisions?"

Prophecy Watch

The World Is Learning to Speak With One Voice

Babel, Global Interconnectedness, and the Final Call

Every day, the news reminds us that we are living in an increasingly interconnected world. Nations are working together to address global conflicts, economic uncertainty, pandemics, climate concerns, cyber threats, and the rapid advancement of artificial intelligence. International cooperation has become the answer to many of the world’s greatest challenges.

There is nothing inherently wrong with nations working together. In fact, cooperation can often prevent suffering and promote peace. Yet, as students of Bible prophecy, we should recognize that Scripture reveals a time when the world will become united in ways never before seen.

The Babel Blueprint

This is not a new idea. The Bible first introduces it in Genesis 11 at the Tower of Babel. Humanity sought unity, not to glorify God, but to exalt itself. Their ambition was to build a society independent of their Creator. God intervened, confusing their languages and scattering the people across the earth.

Genesis 11:6

Today, we are witnessing a different kind of global unity. Advances in technology have removed barriers that once separated nations. Governments can coordinate in real time. Financial systems are interconnected. Information travels around the world in seconds. International organizations influence policies affecting billions of people. Even conversations surrounding ethics, religion, artificial intelligence, and global governance are becoming increasingly unified.

These developments do not, by themselves, fulfill Bible prophecy. However, they demonstrate how the world is becoming capable of the kind of worldwide cooperation described in Revelation.

The book of Revelation repeatedly speaks of events involving “all the world” and “every nation, kindred, tongue, and people.” Revelation 13 describes a time when political, economic, and religious powers will work together, resulting in pressure upon every person regarding worship and allegiance to God. For generations, Seventh-day Adventists have understood that the final conflict will not merely be political or economic. At its heart, it will be a conflict over worship, loyalty, and obedience to God’s Word.

"And when these things begin to come to pass, then look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh."

— Luke 21:28

As we watch these global trends unfold, our response should never be fear or speculation. Jesus did not give prophecy to frighten His people but to prepare them.

While the world is moving toward greater unity, God is also calling together a worldwide people. Revelation 14:6 describes the everlasting gospel being proclaimed to “every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people.” Before Christ returns, God will have a faithful people who lovingly proclaim His final message of hope to the entire world.

Our Choice in the Conflict

The question is not whether the world will unite. Scripture tells us it will. The real question is this: Around whom will we unite? Will our allegiance be shaped by the changing ideas of humanity, or will it remain anchored in Jesus Christ and His unchanging Word?

As we follow current events, let us do so through the lens of Scripture. Let every headline remind us that our confidence is not in governments, economies, or human solutions, but in the soon return of our Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ.

“Even so, come, Lord Jesus.” (Revelation 22:20)
