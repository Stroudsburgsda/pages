Sabbath School

Pride Versus Humility

Sabbath School Lesson — This Week

This week's lesson invites us to take an introspective look at the effects of pride on our relationship with God.

What Is Pride?

Pride is the exalting of self above God and others. According to the Bible, it is a disposition that the Lord detests — Proverbs 16:5.

?

The One-Question Pride Test

"How much do I dislike it when other people snub me, or refuse to take any notice of me, or patronize me, or show off?"

Pride inherently leads to a dislike for others who also exhibit it. If you view yourself as superior, you will be bothered when someone else acts that way toward you. The more offended you become by rude remarks, the more prideful you are.

Those who are humble can easily overlook such slights, while prideful individuals become outraged if their pride is challenged. As long as we maintain a sense of superiority, we cannot truly connect with God. Humility is essential for spiritual growth and for effective missionary work.

Question of the Week

What does the lesson suggest is the only way to obtain a true knowledge of self and overcome pride?

  • By achieving professional excellence and giving a portion of the credit to God.
  • By beholding Christ and drawing closer to Him.
  • Through rigorous self-evaluation and psychological analysis.
  • By comparing our accomplishments to those of people less fortunate.

Answer: B

Come to Sabbath School

What is the trickiest form of pride? Come to Sabbath School to hear the answer — you will be surprised.

Reference: Quintana, H. (2025). Growing in a relationship with God. Pacific Press Publishing Association.

Religious Liberty

Watching the Signs, Strengthening Our Faith

Week of April 13, 2026

This week, two major developments remind us how quickly our world is shifting. Rising tensions involving Iran continue to highlight how fragile global trade systems are — especially through critical routes like the Strait of Hormuz.

At the same time, here in the United States, the federal government convened the final hearing of the Religious Liberty Commission, bringing together national leaders, faith representatives, and policymakers to assess the present and future of religious freedom.

Religious Liberty Commission — Final Hearing

This final hearing reviewed findings from multiple sessions and is expected to contribute to recommendations shaping the future of religious liberty in America. Concerns about faith, freedom, and conscience are no longer distant ideas — they are active discussions at the highest levels of government.

Read the full DOJ article →

"…no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark."
Revelation 13:17

Jesus warned us in Matthew 24 that there would be signs leading up to the end — including unrest, uncertainty, and increasing tension. These are not random moments. They are reminders that we are living in significant times.

While the world sees political and economic challenges, God's people are called to see prophecy unfolding. Current events are not themselves the fulfillment of this prophecy, but they clearly show how systems of influence and control are aligning in ways that were once unimaginable.

The question is not just what is happening around us, but whether we are spiritually awake. Are we grounded in God's Word? Take time this week to pray for discernment, deepen your study, and draw closer to Christ. God is not trying to scare us — but to wake us.

What God's People Should Remember

  • Religious liberty is becoming a major national conversation
  • Economic systems and global trade can shift quickly
  • Prophecy centers on worship and loyalty to God
  • Now is the time to be spiritually prepared
Stewardship Department Spotlight

God Owns It All

This Week's Theme

"The earth is the Lord's, and all its fullness."
Psalm 24:1

Everything we have belongs to God, and we are managers, not owners. Stewardship begins with surrender, not fundraising. When we recognize God as the Source of all we have, our approach to money and resources changes. We become more intentional, more faithful, and more willing to honor Him with our money and in every area of life.

Ask yourself: Am I truly managing God's blessings as His steward?

Once you've reflected, ask God to show you one way to honor Him this week with the resources He has entrusted to you.

There are blessings in store.
