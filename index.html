Week of 4/13/2026

Pride Versus Humility

Have you had an opportunity to get into this week’s lesson? We’re studying the lesson titled Pride Versus Humility. We’re invited to take an introspective look at the effects of pride on our relationship with God.

What is pride? Pride is the exalting of self above God and others. According to the Bible, it is a disposition that the Lord detests (Proverbs 16:5).

Is there a pride test?

Here’s a one-question test to measure how much pride you have. How much do I dislike it when other people snub me, or refuse to take any notice of me, or patronize me, or show off?

Pride inherently leads to a dislike for others who also exhibit pride. Essentially, if you view yourself as superior, you will be bothered when someone else acts that way towards you. The more offended you become by rude remarks and interactions, the more prideful you are. Those who are humble can easily overlook such slights, while prideful individuals become outraged if their pride is challenged.

Humility is essential for spiritual growth and for effective missionary work. As long as we maintain a sense of superiority, we cannot truly connect with God. How can we stand before our Creator while harboring pride?

Religious Liberty

Watching the Signs, Strengthening Our Faith

Rising tensions involving Iran continue to highlight how fragile global trade systems are. Simultaneously, the federal government convened the final hearing of the Religious Liberty Commission to assess the future of religious freedom in America.

Read the full DOJ article here

Jesus warned us in Matthew 24 of unrest and increasing tension. These are reminders that we are living in significant times. While the world sees political and economic challenges, God’s people are called to see prophecy unfolding.

Revelation 13:17 tells us of a time when “no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark.” Current events show how systems of control are aligning in ways once unimaginable.

Key Takeaways

  • Religious liberty is becoming a major national conversation
  • Economic systems and global trade can shift quickly
  • Prophecy centers on worship and loyalty to God
  • Now is the time to be spiritually prepared
