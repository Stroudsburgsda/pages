Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 6/6/2026

Covered by Grace: The Beauty of Repentance and Forgiveness

Few things are more difficult than admitting, “I was wrong.” Our natural instinct is often to justify, minimize, explain, or quietly move on. This week’s lesson, “Forgiveness and Repentance,” invites us into something far more freeing: honest repentance and the transforming grace of God.

Repentance is more than feeling bad about consequences or experiencing temporary guilt. Scripture presents it as a turning, a return to God. Like Israel in Hosea’s appeal, God calls His people not simply to acknowledge failure, but to come back into a restored relationship with Him.

A Perfect Covering

One of the most powerful images in this week’s study is the robe of righteousness. Like Adam and Eve trying to cover themselves with fig leaves, we often attempt our own spiritual solutions: good works, excuses, comparison, busyness, or self-reliance. But our handmade coverings can never accomplish what only Christ can do. God provides the covering we cannot create for ourselves, the perfect righteousness of Jesus.

And what kind of God are we returning to? Not a distant God waiting to condemn. Not a reluctant God withholding mercy. The lesson highlights a God who describes Himself as merciful, gracious, patient, and abounding in steadfast love. Before we even know how to find our way back, God is already extending the invitation home.

That is the heart of the gospel. Repentance does not earn forgiveness. Forgiveness flows from God’s grace. And when we truly experience that grace, repentance becomes less about fear and more about love. Love awakened by the realization that we are fully known, fully forgiven, and fully invited into a renewed relationship with God.

This week, we’re not merely studying about sin management. We’re studying restoration, healing, and walking out of darkness into God’s marvelous light. So carve out time to study deeply, reflect honestly, and allow the Holy Spirit to soften your heart to God’s call.

Food for Thought (for Sabbath discussion):

What is the difference between feeling guilty about sin and experiencing genuine repentance, and how can we recognize when God’s grace is producing real transformation in our lives?

Religious Liberty

The Time Is Short

A Call to Reach Our Community

As we prepare for our End Game: Hope in the Last Days series, the Religious Liberty Team would like to encourage each of us to prayerfully consider the role God has given us in these final moments of earth's history.

As Seventh-day Adventists, we have been entrusted with the everlasting gospel and the Three Angels' Messages of Revelation 14. These messages are not merely doctrines to be understood. They are Heaven's final appeal to a world that is searching for hope, truth, and purpose.

Every day, people around us are struggling with fear, uncertainty, loneliness, brokenness, and confusion. Many have never heard the truths that God has graciously revealed to us. They may never walk into our church on their own, but they may come because someone cared enough to invite them.

The reality is that Heaven is our goal, but God never intended for us to make the journey alone. Jesus commanded His followers, "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations" (Matthew 28:19). The early church grew because ordinary believers shared an extraordinary message. They prayed, they invited, they served, and God added to the church daily.

July 4 Community Outreach Initiative

On July 4, we have an opportunity to take hope into our community. Community outreach is more than handing out flyers. It is an act of love. It is planting seeds for eternity. Every invitation handed out, every conversation, every smile, every prayer offered may be the very thing God uses to reach a soul for His kingdom.

Many times we think someone else will do it. We assume others will show up. But church growth and soul winning happen when every member recognizes that they have a part to play. Some can distribute invitations. Some can pray. Some can encourage. Some can simply be present. Together, we can make a difference.

Our July 4 outreach is not a one-time event. It is the beginning of a continued effort to reach our community with the love of Christ and the truths of His Word. The Religious Liberty Team will also be organizing community outreach efforts in August, September, and October, building relationships, sharing hope, offering prayer, and inviting people to study God's Word. The work of soul winning is not accomplished in a day. Seeds are planted, watered, and nurtured over time. As a church family, we have an opportunity to work together month by month, trusting God to bring the increase and prepare hearts for His soon return.

We are living in a time when prophecy is rapidly fulfilling before our eyes. If there was ever a time to be engaged in God's mission, it is now. The Three Angels' Messages are Heaven's final warning and invitation to humanity, and God is calling His church to share them with urgency, compassion, and love.

The Religious Liberty Team invites each member to join us, not only on July 4, but throughout the months ahead as we seek to make a lasting impact in our community. Whether you can participate once or every month, your presence, prayers, and support matter. Together, let us answer God's call and faithfully carry the Three Angels' Messages to those who need to hear them.

One day, when we stand on the sea of glass and look around the kingdom, may we see people there because we chose to answer God's call. May we see neighbors, friends, coworkers, and complete strangers who became our brothers and sisters in Christ because someone cared enough to reach out.

The harvest is ready. The time is short. Jesus is coming soon.

Let us go.
