Prayer Warriors

What happens when prayer moves from routine to relationship?
In this week’s lesson, “Prayer Warriors,” we look closely at the lives of Moses, Enoch, and Daniel. They didn’t just talk about prayer; they lived it. We also discover that prayer is not an accessory to the Christian life; it is the lifeline.

In crisis and in the ordinary rhythm of life Daniel prayed. He prayed consistently, openly, and without compromise. Even when his life was threatened, his habit of prayer did not change. That kind of faithfulness doesn’t develop overnight; it is built through daily, intentional communion with God.
Enoch’s entire life is summarized in one powerful phrase: “he walked with God.” His relationship wasn’t confined to a specific moment, it was continuous. Prayer, wasn’t just something he did; it was the atmosphere he lived in.

Moses was a leader, intercessor, and friend of God. His prayers weren’t shallow or self-focused. He pleaded for others, he stood in the gap, and trusted God even when the answers were difficult. His life reminds us that prayer is not only about personal connection, but it’s also about intercession and influence.

Daniel, Enoch, and Moses teach us that:
Prayer is consistent. Prayer is relational. Prayer is transformative.

Prayer doesn’t just change situations, it changes people. It deepens trust, clarifies purpose, strengthens faith, and aligns our hearts with God’s will.

Yet many of us struggle with prayer.
Not because prayer is complicated, but because it requires intentionality. We must set aside time, resist distraction, and cultivate the habit of prayer. When we do, the reward will be a living, growing relationship with a God who hears, responds, and draws near.
So this week’s study is more than just one of prayer, it’s an invitation to become a people of prayer.
To move beyond rushed words and into meaningful conversation.
To shift from inconsistency to rhythm.
To experience what it truly means to walk and talk with God.

Stewardship Spotlight

Theme: Where Does It Go?

Malachi (prove Him)

Last week, we were reminded of God’s invitation in Malachi to “prove Him” through faithful returning of tithe and offering.

This week, we want to bring clarity to an important question:
Where do our tithe and offerings go?
In our church structure:

  • All tithe is sent to the Pennsylvania Conference of Seventh-day Adventists to support the broader work of the church—pastors, evangelism, and ministry across the conference.
  • Offerings, specifically Combined Budget, stay right here in our local church.

What does the Combined Budget support?
The Combined Budget helps meet the ongoing needs of our church, including:

  • Utilities (electricity, trash, etc.)
  • Sabbath meals and fellowship
  • Sabbath School quarterlies
  • Departmental ministries and events
  • Day-to-day operations of the church
    Additionally, there are opportunities to give to specific areas such as:
  • AY (Youth Ministries)
  • Family Life
  • Benevolent Fund
  • And other ministries that serve our church and community

Why This Matters
Returning tithe is an act of obedience and trust in God.
Giving offering is also a call from God—as seen in Malachi—and while He does not specify an amount, it is an invitation for each of us to give willingly and faithfully to support His house locally.
If we return tithe but do not give offering, the local church does not have the resources needed to function, serve, and grow.

A Shared Responsibility
Each of us plays a part in sustaining and strengthening God’s church.
When we give:

  • We support the mission
  • We meet real needs
  • We create space for ministry to happen
  • We impact lives—right here in our church and in our community

Our Encouragement This Week
As you continue to prove God in your giving, consider not only your tithe, but also how you can support your local church through offering.
Trust Him. Honor Him. And watch how He blesses:

  • You
  • Your family
  • Your church
  • And your community
    Because stewardship is not just about what we give—it’s about who we trust.
    May God richly bless your faithfulness as you trust Him.

Family Life

Last Week’s Sabbath School lesson, “How to Study the Bible,” gave us a glimpse into the purpose of Bible study — the how, when, and where — the double blessings it brings, and the sweetness of God’s Word that brings healing to our souls and transformation to our characters.

Over the next 3 weeks, the Family Life Department will bring you a Bible study designed not to condemn, but to pour out Living Water — water meant to quench your thirst, answer your questions, strengthen your relationships with God and with others, satisfy the longing of your heart, and draw you into a deeper understanding of His love and truth.

Together, we will explore practical and biblical topics such as spiritual protection through the whole armor of God, building healthy Christ-centered relationships before and within marriage, understanding submission from God’s perspective, how husbands are called to love their wives, and safeguarding our hearts and minds in a world filled with temptation.

This Bible study is for the young and the young at heart. We have prepared it with the entire church family in mind.

So we invite you to:
Bring your Bible.
Bring your notebook.
Bring your questions.
And most of all, bring an open heart.

We look forward to studying, growing, and journeying together in God’s Word.
