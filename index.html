Stewardship Department Spotlight

Stewardship is worshiping God through the faithful management of all He has entrusted to us. It goes beyond money and includes how we honor Him with our Treasure, Time, Temple, Talent, and Testimony.

This year, the Stewardship Department will focus on helping our church family develop a broader biblical understanding of stewardship, grow in faithfulness and intentionality, highlight practical stewardship principles, engage both adults and youth, and encourage testimonies of God’s provision and faithfulness.

Embracing the Five Key Areas:

Treasure — Faithfully managing the financial resources God has entrusted to us.
Time — Using our time wisely and intentionally for God’s glory.
Temple — Caring for our bodies as the temple of the Holy Spirit.
Talent — Developing and using our God-given gifts in service to Him.
Testimony — Sharing God’s faithfulness and the impact of living as faithful stewards.

Please keep the Stewardship Department in prayer as we seek to encourage our church family to honor God in every area of life.

Sabbath School Midweek Momentum

Welcome to the first Sabbath School Midweek Momentum. These weekly messages are designed to encourage us to engage with the sabbath school lesson. This quarter’s lessons are especially personal and have the potential to guide us into a deeper relationship with God.

This Week's Lesson: "To Know God"

This week takes us on a journey to understand God's character, which is the absolute foundation for a strong relationship with Him. The Bible provides the clearest picture of who God is, actively countering Satan's ancient lies that God is untrustworthy, does not want what is best for us, and is keeping secrets.

Facets of God's Character (Monday – Wednesday):

God Is Holy

Holiness is the core of who God is. Being holy means He is completely separated from evil and 100% good from beginning to end. Knowing and understanding that God is completely holy is crucial because it reassures us that His immense power (omnipotence) and knowledge (omniscience) are perfectly good, not tyrannical.

God Is Love

The Bible doesn't just say God is loving; it declares that "God is love". This is a pure, selfless, and deeply relational covenant love. The greatest ultimate expression of this radical love was God sending His Son, Jesus, to die for sinners and bridge the gap between humanity and Himself.

God in Creation

Looking at the creation story, the Bible uses two different names to give us a perfectly balanced picture of God. Elohim describes the almighty, transcendent Creator who speaks the universe into existence. In contrast, Yahweh reveals a personal, intimate God who stoops down to breathe life directly into humanity. Together, they show a God who is both infinitely powerful and beautifully close.

Test Your Knowledge

Question: Why is God's holiness considered central to His other attributes, such as His omnipotence (being all-powerful) and omniscience (being all-knowing)?
