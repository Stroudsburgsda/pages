Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 6/27/2026

Into Eternity: Keeping Our Eyes on the Promise

For the past 13 weeks, we have been studying lessons on growing in a relationship with God. These practical and transformative lessons were designed to enrich our spiritual journey. This week’s lesson, “Into Eternity,” presents us with the much-needed reminder that this world is not the end of our story.

Scripture tells us that eternity has been placed in our hearts. Deep down, we know there must be more than this cycle of pain, struggle, and temporary joys. This week’s study lifts our eyes beyond the chaos of earth and anchors them in the certainty of Christ’s return. One day, the trumpet will sound. The dead in Christ will rise. The faithful will be caught up to meet Jesus in the air. And for the first time, faith will become sight.

Unbroken Fellowship

We will finally see the one we’ve prayed to, trusted, wept before, and waited for. No longer would we be separated by distance. There will be no more wondering and no more waiting. We will live in a place untouched by sin, untouched by death, untouched by grief. No tears. No suffering. No loss. Only joy, restoration, and unbroken fellowship with God.

But perhaps the greatest reward of eternity is not the city itself. It is Jesus. To see Him. To worship Him. To thank Him personally for His sacrifice, His patience, His grace, and His faithfulness through every trial. The Lamb who led us here on Earth will still be our Shepherd in heaven. That truth should change how we live. If we believe that eternity is real, our choices today matter. Our relationship with God matters. Our witness matters. Our preparation matters.

This final lesson of the quarter invites us to live with the end in view. So this week, take time to study deeply. Let the promise of eternity strengthen your faith, steady your hope, and renew your longing for Christ’s soon return. Because the best is not behind us. It’s ahead.

Food for Thought (for Sabbath discussion):

If heaven is ultimately about being with Jesus, how should that reality shape the way we live, prioritize, and prepare right now?

Outreach Preparation

Before We Hand Out a Single Flyer

Building Connections with Sincerity & Love

On July 4, many of us will walk through the streets of Stroudsburg wearing our church shirts, carrying gift bags, and inviting people to upcoming events. For some, that will feel natural. For others, it may feel uncomfortable.

The truth is that most people are not afraid of talking about Jesus. They are afraid of being rejected. What if someone says no? What if they ignore me? What if I don't know what to say? These are normal concerns, but they remind us of an important truth: our job is not to convince anyone. Our job is simply to be available for God to use.

Recognizing the Needs Around Us

The person standing in front of you may have had a difficult week. They may be carrying burdens no one else knows about. They may be lonely, discouraged, grieving, or searching for answers. We often assume people are not interested when, in reality, many are simply waiting for someone to show genuine kindness.

Five Simple Principles to Remember:

1. Start with a Smile, Not a Speech

You do not need a rehearsed presentation. A smile, a greeting, and a friendly attitude often open more doors than a lengthy introduction. People respond to warmth and sincerity.

2. Offer the Gift Bag First

The bag is a gift, not a sales pitch. Simply introduce yourself and offer the materials. Let people know that your church is sharing free resources and information about upcoming community events.

"We're from the Stroudsburg Seventh-day Adventist Church and we're sharing a free gift with our community today."

3. Let the T-Shirt Start the Conversation

Our shirts were designed to create curiosity. If someone comments on your shirt or asks a question, that becomes a natural opportunity to share why we are there and what our church is doing in the community. Simply explain that we are sharing hope, prayer, and information about upcoming community events.

4. Offer Prayer Naturally

One of the greatest gifts we can offer is prayer. A simple question such as, "Is there anything we can pray about for you?" may open the door to a conversation God has already been preparing. Some may share a burden. Others may simply thank you. Either way, they will know someone cared enough to ask.

5. Leave Them with an Invitation, Not Pressure

Invite them to End Game. Encourage them to scan the QR code. Let them know they are welcome. Then trust God with the results. The Holy Spirit is responsible for changing hearts. We are responsible for being faithful.

Not every conversation will be long. Not every person will stop. Not every flyer will be read immediately. But every encounter is an opportunity to reflect the character of Christ.

If declined: Wish them a happy Fourth of July and move on graciously.
If accepted: Thank them for their time.
If shared: Listen carefully and offer prayer.

Success is not measured by how many people stop. Success is measured by whether we represent Jesus well.
