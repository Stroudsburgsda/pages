Sabbath School Momentum

Lesson for Sabbath 7/11/2026

More Than a Symbol: Rediscovering the Power of the Cross

In Paul's day, the cross was an instrument of humiliation. It was a symbol of shame and a reminder of suffering and death. Yet Paul boldly declared: "The message of the cross is foolishness to those who are perishing, but to us who are being saved it is the power of God."

When God's wisdom doesn't make sense

Have you ever noticed that God often works in ways we wouldn't choose?

  • We pray for immediate answers, but He teaches us patience.
  • We ask Him to remove the storm, and instead He strengthens us to walk through it.
  • We expect power to look like victory, influence, and control. God reveals His greatest power through sacrifice, humility, and unconditional love.

The Cross reminds us that God's ways are often different from ours, but they are always better.

The Cross changes how we see ourselves

One of the greatest lies we can believe is that we must somehow earn God's approval. The Cross tells a different story. It reminds us that our achievements, failures, education, status, or past mistakes never determined our value. Our worth was settled at Calvary. Every time we look at the Cross, we're reminded that grace always has the final word.

The Cross changes how we see others

The church in Corinth struggled with division. People aligned themselves behind personalities, opinions, and preferences. Sound familiar?

The Cross has a way of leveling the ground. At Calvary, there are no VIP sections. No one stands above another. Every believer comes the same way: through grace. When Christ becomes our focus, pride begins to lose its grip. Unity becomes possible because we remember that every one of us is simply a recipient of mercy.
