Lesson for Sabbath 6/13/2026

When Life Doesn’t Go According to Plan

A health diagnosis. A financial struggle. The loss of someone we love. A prayer that seems unanswered. In moments like these, it’s easy to wonder where God is and why He seems silent. Few things test our faith like setbacks. This week’s lesson takes us into the stories of people who faced disappointment, suffering, confusion, and loss, yet discovered that God had not abandoned them.

The disciples were caught in a violent storm on the Sea of Galilee. While they feared for their lives, Jesus slept peacefully in the boat. Their question, “Do you not care?” is one many believers have echoed in their own storms. The lesson reminds us that God's apparent silence is not evidence of His absence. Often, He is working in ways we cannot yet see.

Reaching Through the Storm

Then there is the woman who suffered for years, exhausted every resource, and still found no relief. Instead of giving up, she pressed through the crowd to reach Jesus. Her story reminds us that faith is not the absence of struggle; it is choosing to reach for Christ in the midst of it.

Few people have experienced the deep loss and confusion that Job faced. Even when he could not understand God’s plans, he refused to abandon his faith. Through his suffering, Job gained a deeper understanding of God’s grace and character than he had known before.

On the road to Emmaus, the disciples thought their hopes had been shattered. What felt like the greatest setback in history was actually part of God's greatest victory. Jesus was walking beside them long before they recognized Him. The same is true for us. Our setbacks may not make sense today. We may not see the full picture. But Scripture repeatedly points us to a God who remains faithful, whose love remains constant, and who can bring purpose even out of pain.

In studying this week’s lesson, you may discover that the greatest threat to your faith is not the setback itself, but forgetting that Jesus is still in the boat, still on the road, and still at work.

Question of the Week

During the storm on the Sea of Galilee, Jesus was sleeping at the stern on a pillow. What is the significance of this specific location in the boat?

A. The stern was the area reserved for honored guests to rest away from the spray of the water.
B. The stern contained the only storage area where a pillow could be kept dry.
C. The stern was the most stable part of the fishing boat during a heavy storm.
D. The stern was the position of the boat’s driver, implying Jesus was “at the wheel” even while asleep.
Correct Answer: D
Food for Thought (for Sabbath discussion):

When facing setbacks, what most determines whether your faith grows stronger or weaker, and how can you learn to recognize God's presence even when His purposes are not yet clear? How can Scripture help strengthen your trust?

Religious Liberty

Following the Pattern of the Early Church

A Call to Action for the July 4 Community Outreach

One of the most remarkable characteristics of the early Christian church was its commitment to reaching people where they were. The book of Acts reveals a church that did not remain within its walls. Believers went into cities, marketplaces, homes, and public spaces sharing the hope of Christ. Through personal connections, acts of kindness, Bible-centered conversations, and the power of the Holy Spirit, the gospel spread rapidly throughout the known world. Today, God is calling His church to follow that same model.

We are living in prophetic times. The world is experiencing growing uncertainty, moral confusion, social division, economic instability, and increasing restrictions on freedom of conscience. At such a time as this, the Three Angels' Messages of Revelation 14 must be shared more urgently than ever before. The message of salvation through Christ, obedience to God's commandments, and preparation for His soon return is not merely information. It is Heaven's final appeal to a world that is searching for hope, truth, and purpose.

A Prophetic Milestone: America at 250

This year also marks a significant milestone in American history: the approaching 250th anniversary of the United States. Students of Bible prophecy recognize that America occupies a unique place in Revelation 13. A nation founded upon principles of civil and religious liberty has been greatly used by God to advance the gospel throughout the world. Yet prophecy also warns of future challenges to those freedoms. As we reflect on 250 years of American history, we are reminded that now is the time to strengthen our witness, share truth in love, and help our communities understand God's Word before the final events unfold.

For this reason, the Religious Liberty Team is inviting every church member, ministry leader, church officer, department, ministry team, and family to participate in the July 4 Community Outreach initiative. This is not simply another church event. It is an opportunity for the entire church family to unite in mission and connect with our community in a meaningful way.

The early church grew because every believer saw themselves as a missionary. The same can be true today. Whether you distribute literature, offer prayer, engage in conversation, invite someone to our upcoming End Game prophecy series, or simply show Christ's love through kindness, your participation matters. As we prepare for July 4 and the months of outreach that will follow, let us pray for open doors, receptive hearts, and a renewed burden for souls. The harvest is still plentiful, and God is calling His people to labor while there is time.
