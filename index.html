Week of 4/13/2026

Pride Versus Humility

Have you had an opportunity to get into this week’s lesson? We’re studying the lesson titled Pride Versus Humility. We’re invited to take an introspective look at the effects of pride on our relationship with God.

What is pride? Pride is the exalting of self above God and others. According to the Bible, it is a disposition that the Lord detests (Proverbs 16:5).

Is there a pride test?

"Here’s a one-question test to measure how much pride you have: How much do I dislike it when other people snub me, or refuse to take any notice of me, or patronize me, or show off?"

Pride inherently leads to a dislike for others who also exhibit pride. Essentially, if you view yourself as superior, you will be bothered when someone else acts that way towards you. The more offended you become by rude remarks and interactions, the more prideful you are.

Those who are humble can easily overlook such slights, while prideful individuals become outraged if their pride is challenged. It's important to realize that as long as we maintain a sense of superiority, we cannot truly connect with God. How can we stand before our Creator while harboring pride? Humility is essential for spiritual growth and for effective missionary work.

Reference: Quintana, H. (2025). Growing in a relationship with God. Pacific Press Publishing Association.

Stewardship Spotlight

Theme: God Owns It All

“The earth is the Lord’s, and all its fullness.”

— Psalm 24:1

Everything we have belongs to God, and we are managers, not owners. Stewardship begins with surrender, not fundraising. When we recognize God as the Source of all we have, our approach to money and resources changes. We become more intentional, more faithful, and more willing to honor Him with our money and in every area of life.

Ask yourself: Am I truly managing God’s blessings as His steward?

Once you’ve reflected on that question, ask God to show you one way to honor Him this week with the resources He has entrusted to you. There are blessings in store.

Have questions? Need support? Reach out to the Stewardship Director, Sister Ranelli Williams.

Religious Liberty

Watching the Signs, Strengthening Our Faith

This week, two major developments remind us how quickly our world is shifting. Rising tensions involving Iran continue to highlight how fragile global trade systems are, especially through critical routes like the Strait of Hormuz. At the same time, here in the United States, the federal government convened the final hearing of the Religious Liberty Commission, bringing together national leaders, faith representatives, and policymakers to assess the present and future of religious freedom.

This final hearing reviewed findings from multiple sessions and is expected to contribute to recommendations shaping the future of religious liberty in America. This shows that concerns about faith, freedom, and conscience are no longer distant ideas, but active discussions at the highest levels of government.

Jesus warned us in Matthew 24 that there would be signs leading up to the end, including unrest, uncertainty, and increasing tension. These are not random moments. They are reminders that we are living in significant times. While the world sees political and economic challenges, God’s people are called to see prophecy unfolding.

The Bible tells us in Revelation 13:17 that a time will come when “no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark.” While current events are not themselves the fulfillment of this prophecy, they clearly show how systems of influence, control, and even religious discussion are aligning in ways that were once unimaginable.

This week, the question is not just what is happening around us, but whether we are spiritually awake. Are we grounded in God’s Word? Are we preparing our hearts to stand? Take time this week to pray for discernment, deepen your study, and draw closer to Christ. God is not trying to scare us, but to wake us.

What God’s People Should Remember

  • Religious liberty is becoming a major national conversation
  • Economic systems and global trade can shift quickly
  • Prophecy centers on worship and loyalty to God
  • Now is the time to be spiritually prepared
